Context

Tiro.health is a healthtech company with a digital medical management product. Our team built a conversational AI assistant in the document builder.

The main goal was to create an AI-assistant directly in Tiro.health, eliminating context switching and streamlining document creation.

Research

We regularly meet with our main B2B customers and collect in-app feedback after users create documents. One of the most frequent requests has been adding an AI assistant to the document creation flow.

Currently, users leave Tiro.health to use external AI tools, copying and pasting content to get help. This context switching slows down work, and breaks flow.

To better understand this behavior, we synthesized feedback and conducted additional usability testing to observe how users interact with AI tools.

The prompting gap

During testing, we observed that most users struggled to formulate effective prompts. As a result, they often received unsatisfactory outputs and blamed the AI itself, when the real issue was the lack of guidance on how to write prompts.

The "unknown capabilities" problem

Users tended to rely on a narrow set of AI actions because they weren't sure what the AI could actually help with. Many participants explicitly said they didn't know the full range of tasks the AI could support, which limited overall value.

Metrics

After gaining a solid understanding of the problem space, we distilled our insights into two primary HMWs. We linked these questions with relevant metrics to define what success would look like.

AI CSAT

HMW provide contextual guidance that helps clinicians create effective prompts, improving AI output quality and user satisfaction?

AI adoption

HMW surface AI capabilities contextually so clinicians can discover the full range of actions, maximizing the AI's adoption?

Design

I designed end-to-end document AI-native features, with decisions focused on two main objectives: helping users craft effective prompts through contextual guidance, and surfacing the AI's full range of capabilities.

Impact

  • 73% AI adoption, with 78% AI CSAT
  • We won 10+ new clients, signed specifically after an AI-powered features trial
  • AI-native features are now leveraged by our sales team as a key differentiator to attract and win new clients

Reflection

Over the years, I've been building a library of AI design patterns in my spare time, which proved incredibly handy during this project. Engaging with Chris Butler's AI Product Design Patterns course and reading UX for AI further strengthened my perspective.

I had close collaboration with AI engineers and the dev team to explore and evaluate AI capabilities. This experience reaffirmed that clear communication, collaboration, and adaptability are essential for a successful project.
